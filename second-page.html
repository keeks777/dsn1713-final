Biography

Suzanne Collins, born on August 10, 1962, in Hartford, Connecticut, is a bestselling American author best known for her *Hunger Games* trilogy and *Underland Chronicles*. After graduating from Indiana University and earning a master's degree in dramatic writing from NYU, she began her career writing for Nickelodeon shows in the 1990s. Her *Hunger Games* trilogy—*The Hunger Games* (2008), *Catching Fire* (2009), and *Mockingjay* (2010)—explores themes of survival and social inequality, selling over 50 million copies and inspiring a successful film series. Collins has received numerous awards and continues to engage readers of all ages with her compelling storytelling.
